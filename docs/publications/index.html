How Semiotic Communication Occurs in Current AutomobilesDraft

Published 08.11.23

Over the past decades, drivers have become familiar with touchscreens in cars. Some drivers follow the best practices and mostly ignore the screen, only glancing at it for navigation, and only interacting with it when stationary. This document is not a report on the cause of accidents, and we won’t argue that it’s because of the touchscreen that road fatality has increased — and yet the trend is obvious and the critics are loud: these screens are distracting and difficult to use. Manufacturers are naive if they believe people won’t use the built-in display while driving. And yet! People are getting used to screens, they are becoming an accepted technology, no longer a gimmick. And if drivers don’t use the built-in touchscreen of the car’s infotainment system, they bring their own: their smartphone.
